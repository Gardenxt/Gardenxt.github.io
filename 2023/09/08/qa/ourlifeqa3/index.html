【ourlife】Q＆A 2023May

发表于 2023-09-08 更新于 2025-07-27 分类于 游戏

Hello everyone. Welcome to the May 2023 Patreon Q&A. And this month is the one year anniversary of us starting this whole Q&A thing! I can’t believe it’s been that long already, but it also feels like we’ve always been doing this. And right now I’m sitting in my new place that I recently moved to. It’s been a few days since Baxter has come out. Everything is mostly settled, but there is so much to do ahead of this that is like, I don’t even know how I’m going to fit it all in. So much to share, but it’s all very good and exciting. But for right now, I’m just here to answer all the questions that got sent in over this past month, and they were a lot of them. So let’s get started.

The first question had the first comment, had various questions, and some of them I couldn’t answer for spoiler reasons or because they weren’t determined yet. But for most of the questions, it starts with: In the beta, we can see that Baxter refers to his parents as mother and father instead of using more casual terms. Is this due to his relationship with his parents not being intimate enough, or is it a personal preference? And what does Baxter prefer his children to call him?

For Baxter, it is a personal preference, as I’ve said before, and I’ll say again, he’s very pretentious. And so he calls his parents, mother and father. And for what he would want his kids to call him, honestly, he probably would just want to be dad. He wouldn’t insist on his children very formally calling him father.

And the next one of this comment is: Did Baxter have an ulterior motive when he answered Scott’s question about whether he was married with being “Entirely single, in fact”, when he could have just said, “I’m not married yet”, which would have worked just as well?

And well, basically, he didn’t want further questions about his relationship status. Obviously, if the two of you dated in the past, there could be an implication that he’s informing the MC that he hasn’t moved on and started a new relationship after what he did to them. But generally, he just sort of wants to cut it off there like, “No, I’m not married, I’m not even dating anyone. This isn’t a topic that you can continue to discuss about.”

Do you think it’s likely that Baxter would choose a photo of either his children or himself with the MC as his phone background?

Um, probably not. He’s not the kind of guy that sort of sentimentally customize his phone. He’d probably just keep it a very simple, probably black and white photograph of nothing in particular.

Do you think Baxter was making Dad jokes in the future?

No, probably not. He’s got his own weird sense of humor, and he’ll probably stick to that even if he became a dad.

How’s Baxter doing with ice skating?

Not terrible, but not amazing. He’s, you know, a better dancer on regular ground, but he can at least stay upright when ice skating.

The last one for this topic is: Would Baxter be willing to accompany the MC during birth if they asked him?

And yes, he would definitely be there if they wanted him to.

How does everyone feel about sharing clothes with their partner?

I’m not entirely sure who you mean by everybody. In Our Life 1 or Our Life 2? I would say generally all the leads are fine with sharing clothes. Sorry, that’s not very specific.

What is your process for writing a route? Do you have a general idea of how the plot is going to go and then consider different choices the player will make? Has the process changed between your different games?

The process definitely has changed between games, but it’s more because other games work very differently than other games. Like the Our Life Series and the XOXO Droplets series, they all consist of a collection of disconnected short stories for the most part. There’s a little– there’s some plot overall story in most of them, but for the most part, you have to create them in a way very different than, say, something like Lake of Voices or A Foretold Affair, where it is much more about singular, longer stories. My process for making a game like Our Life is that I decide how many, you know, events there are going to be overall. I try to decide like what balance I think is, like how many I want to be entirely fun, how many I want to be more dramatic and emotional. Sort of imagine the kinds of locations or situations that sort of evoke the seasonal feel that I’m going for. And I just make a list of all these things that I’d like to happen somewhere at some point. And then once I have a good strong idea for, you know, a short story, a full Moment, I’m totally fine just making it and, you know, putting in some of those ideas and then just continuing to work through the list of stuff I want, sort of keeping the balance between lighthearted and more dramatic. And I don’t have to have every single Moment already planned out before I start writing. And usually, you know, I’ll only have 2 or 3 fully formed ideas before I start writing for a Step. And then the future Steps way later on, I’ll have barely planned out at all, just sort of general ideas of how to continue the themes I’m starting in the Step before it. But when it comes to a storyline that’s not broken up like that, then I do try to have things planned out from beginning to the end. And usually the choices are planned out along with it, and I’m not going back to sort of consider what choices should be there. But there is always room to add more choices while actually creating the story if I think of something else that would be good during that process.

Does she continue to be the kind of person to have fictional crushes as she grows up?

Tamarack mostly likes Saturday morning cartoon kind of things as a child, and the types of characters she likes are probably the main characters they usually have where, you know, they’re not the best, but they’re trying their best and they get the most screen time. So that’s probably who she’d end up being the most fond of. And as she gets older, she probably would still more jokingly be like super in love with the fictional characters, but she wouldn’t really count them as, like, actual crushes.

Thank you for all your hard work. I hope everything related to the Baxter launch and your move goes smoothly. Thank you very much. We get to see how Cove’s feelings and comfort with physical stuff grows, but I was wondering how the leads feel about non sexual or even romantic physical contact, like hugs, hand-holding, etcetera?

While Cove and Derek are shyer about that when they are younger, Derek, as long as it’s not overtly romantic, is usually fine with physical comfort. But once it kind of seems romantic, he can get nervous. Cove is obviously very nervous, even if it seems unromantic, if he’s crushing on the MC. And Baxter is completely comfortable with any kind of physical contact, and it can be know platonic, romantic, or intimate.

He was still in California. The moved seemed like a very big deal to him, but it was still within the same state, and realistically for adults was quite a small move.

They definitely would never become a couple. Realistically, Shiloh is way too difficult as a person to be with someone like Liz. But I mean, if you want a head-canon that one of them did have a crush on the other, at some point, that’s totally fine.

I think I know why, but why is Darren so sensitive to girls?

And there’s a second question: What’s your process when deciding how we’re able to respond to characters? Do you often find it difficult to think of different responses?

And my process is usually going with sort of the first things that come to mind in this situation, kind of what’s specific to what’s happening right then, and then I usually expand it with more variations of things that maybe could go in a lot of situations but would still be good to have. And then I you know, I’m always trying to balance out how many are positive and negative and whether or not there’s nonverbal options. And it’s usually not hard to think of options, but it can be hard to remember sort of every different type of option that people, you know, would want to have, and that is usually on a menu.

I’m glad you like them. And the two of them met at college and they attended college in California. Hobbies they have are they both like gardening. Noelani likes to cook and Pam likes to bake, and Noelani does still love to surf, though she doesn’t have a lot of time to do it. And Pam likes to watch Noelani surf. For their jobs, Lani is a manager at a very expensive boutique, and Pam works for a company doing software engineering stuff.

For the sake of the game’s story and all the different MCs that could be playing, the main, I guess, premise that they wouldn’t like about the MC is left up to the player, and so it’s never specifically mentioned by name. But it could be any number of things because they are very terrible, and basically don’t like anyone who is not white, straight, cis, college educated, born in the USA; and not neurodivergent, not adopted– like anything, they could definitely have something against you for. You can’t win with people like them, and you wouldn’t want to because they’re really just the worst, honestly.

Thank you. I hope you have a good day too. And it is specific to one character. You can’t be best friends with both because the point of the best friend system is to pick a favorite. If you just make them equal in your mind again, that’s kind of defeated the purpose of having an extra high level beyond having them both just be friends. So yes, if you’re best friends with a character, they are your best friend, and that means you prefer them over your other friends. And if you like them both equally, then you just have to say you can’t pick a favorite best friend. And sorry about that.

And then Derek would be, you know, the number one fan kind of end of the spectrum, where he would be super thrilled for them, he’d be excited about it, he’d be happy to be mentioned and to point to them and say, “Hey, did you know that my partner or friend is in this cool, exciting thing?” Or, you know, if they were on the TV, they’d be like, “I know them.” He would be happy to go to like cool celebrity parties or events with them and all that.

Like there already are other writers who have helped with things before, but I still have to create the entire draft. I have to edit whatever’s written. I’m very involved in the programming and it just takes up a lot of mental energy and space in my brain and what I’m thinking about, and I really can’t have that be in there taking attention away from other things. And if I were to just fully say, “I’m not even going to think about it, you someone else just make it happen”, I don’t really think it would end up being what people think of when they think of Our Life content. And I just don’t want there to be content for the sake of content. I would have to personally make sure that it is in line with everything else, and I’m not able to do that even, if I’m not the one writing it.
